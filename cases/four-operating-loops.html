Four Operating Loops

A single-threaded consignment program was redesigned into four concurrent control loops so demand signal, slot economics, reorder logic, and billing-state accuracy could improve independently.

Open modelBack to cases
What this proves

Process architecture under live operating constraints.

This is the strongest evidence that the work was not inventory administration. It was a control-system build for a regulated technical service environment.

The issue was one overloaded attention path.

Demand signals, physical capacity, vendor behavior, reorder logic, and billing-state tracking competed for the same operational attention. The fix was to split the work into clear loops with distinct inputs, outputs, and control logic.

SignalFloor friction appeared before formal shortage reports.Supervisors saw wrong, missing, unnecessary, or difficult-to-find items first.
CapacityVending and storage space were finite.Each item needed to earn its footprint.
StateBilling transitions needed control.First-batch/prepaid items needed clean transition to consignment.
