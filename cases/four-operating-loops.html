Executive summary

Live service work needed separated control paths.

The program had multiple operating signals competing for attention: floor feedback, physical capacity, reorder timing, billing-state transitions, vendor/spec constraints, and shortage escalation. The fix was to separate those signals into visible control loops with clearer inputs, outputs, and follow-up logic.

Operating context

EnvironmentAerospace manufacturing service delivery.Embedded technical operations support in a regulated production environment.
Scale5 buildings, 17 vending systems, 1,500+ active SKUs.Enough complexity to require operating controls, not only manual follow-up.
StakeholdersPlant users, suppliers, receiving, and internal support.Cross-functional coordination was part of the operating load.

Operating model

The operating model separated daily service noise into distinct control paths: floor feedback, slot capacity, replenishment logic, billing-state movement, vendor/spec governance, and shortage escalation.
