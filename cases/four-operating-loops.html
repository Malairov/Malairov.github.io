Four Operating Loops

A single-threaded consignment program was redesigned into four concurrent control loops so demand signal, slot economics, reorder logic, and billing-state accuracy could improve independently.

What this proves Process architecture under live operating constraints.

This is the strongest evidence that the work was not inventory administration. It was a control-system build for a regulated technical service environment.

The problem was not one shortage. It was one overloaded attention path.

Demand signals, physical capacity, vendor behavior, reorder logic, and billing-state tracking were competing for the same operational attention. That made the system hard to audit, hard to tune, and vulnerable to recurring blind spots.

SignalFloor friction arrived faster than formal reports.Supervisors saw wrong, missing, unnecessary, or difficult-to-find items first.
ControlVending slots were expensive and finite.Every slot needed to earn its space through service value and draw behavior.
StateOnboarding and billing states needed control.First-batch/prepaid items needed clean transition to standard consignment billing.
