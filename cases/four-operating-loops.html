Executive summary

A live service program needed separate control loops, not more manual follow-up.

The work supported a production-critical industrial supply program in an aerospace manufacturing environment. The operating challenge was not simply replenishment; it was weak visibility across demand signal, capacity, reorder logic, lifecycle state, supplier constraints, and issue escalation.

Operating context

EnvironmentAerospace manufacturing service delivery.Embedded technical operations support inside a regulated production environment.
Program scale5 buildings, 17 vending systems, 1,500+ active SKUs.High enough complexity to require process controls and operating visibility.
StakeholdersPlant users, supervisors, suppliers, receiving, and internal support teams.Cross-functional coordination was part of the operating load.

Problem and constraints

Signals were fragmented. Floor issues, service gaps, supplier behavior, lead-time risk, and billing-state transitions appeared as separate problems, but they affected the same production-critical service model.

ConstraintPhysical capacity was limited.Vending and storage positions needed slot-economics review.
ConstraintSubstitution was not purely commercial.Vendor/spec governance had to respect customer and OEM constraints.
ConstraintNew items had weak history.No-history items needed exception logic, not automatic dead-demand treatment.
ConstraintIssues needed closure visibility.MWO shortage events required traceable escalation and follow-up.
