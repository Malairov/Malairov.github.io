Executive summary

I made technical delivery visible enough to govern.

Over just under three years (2022–2025) I managed a transportation-safety and smart-infrastructure program delivered in three phases: an AI-enabled pedestrian crossing detection system, an equipment-monitoring platform for the deployed field units, and a micromobility violation-detection module. Field reliability, stakeholder visibility, installation readiness, and measurable deployment outcomes mattered in every phase. My value was not engineering authorship; it was converting technical uncertainty into delivery controls: workstream readiness, issue and risk visibility, sponsor-facing status, pilot-readiness logic, and scale-transition discipline.

The implementation environment included AI-enabled pedestrian, vehicle, and micromobility detection; warning components; projected road-marking visibility; lighting and signage elements; V2I-readiness considerations; and integration logic for Smart City / ITS environments.

Program phases

The program moved through three delivery phases, each with its own team composition, technical risk profile, and readiness challenges. Across all three, the operating need was the same: a clear governance layer showing what was ready, blocked, decision-dependent, or exposed to field-condition risk.

Phase 1 · 2022Field detection systemAI-enabled pedestrian crossing detection: hardware selection, dataset collection, prototype build, city pilot, feedback collection, and transition to scaled deployment with a coordinated installation crew.
Phase 2 · 2023–2024Equipment-monitoring platformA remote-diagnostics platform for the deployed field units: concept design, frontend and backend build, equipment connection, and client-facing testing and refinement.
Phase 3 · 2024–2025Micromobility violation moduleAn e-scooter violation-detection module built on the field system: dataset collection, detection refinement, and validation of hard cases such as walked versus ridden scooters.

Delivery architecture / technical workstreams

I kept the delivery view organized around the technical streams that had to become ready together. This is the PM signal: I did not need to own each engineering decision to make the system governable.

01Detection logicI coordinated delivery visibility around pedestrian, vehicle, and micromobility detection behavior, movement-direction logic, and readiness risks around false positives / false negatives.
02Field hardware readinessI helped structure readiness visibility across projection, lighting, warning signs, flashing warning beacons, camera placement, and real-road visibility conditions.
03Violation / event logicI supported implementation governance around driver non-yield scenarios, the micromobility violation-detection module, incident capture, and event/archive requirements — including hard detection cases such as walked versus ridden scooters.
04ITS / Smart City integrationI helped keep integration dependencies visible for regional ITS, Safe City, Smart City environments, and V2I-readiness considerations.
05Pilot environment readinessI helped connect validation logic to high-risk deployment contexts such as unregulated crossings, school zones, limited-visibility areas, transit stops, and other field-risk locations.
06Sponsor governanceI maintained visibility over readiness, risks, dependencies, decisions, and validation status so sponsor-facing execution could move from prototype toward pilot validation and scale transition.
