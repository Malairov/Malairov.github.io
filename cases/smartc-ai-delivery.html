Executive summary

Technical delivery work had to become visible and governable.

The SmartC work involved AI-enabled transportation safety, hardware readiness, computer-vision/software behavior, QA validation, field integration, and sponsor-controlled governance. My contribution was delivery leadership: making risks, dependencies, readiness gaps, and sponsor decisions visible enough to move recovery work toward pilot validation and scale transition.

Recovery context

I worked through reliability and readiness gaps that created delivery risk across several technical streams. The operating need was not more decorative reporting; it was a clear governance layer showing what was ready, blocked, decision-dependent, or exposed to field-condition risk.

Risk patternReadiness ambiguitySeveral workstreams could appear active while unresolved defects, dependencies, or validation gaps still affected pilot confidence.
Delivery needCommon visibility layerSponsors and delivery teams needed a shared view of scope, status, readiness, and decisions.
Governance constraintSponsor-controlled changeScope, schedule, and budget-impacting changes required visible decision control rather than informal drift.
