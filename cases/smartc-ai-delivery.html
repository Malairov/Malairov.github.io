Executive summary

Implementation governance across technical recovery and scale transition.

This case shows delivery leadership for AI-enabled transportation technology work involving recovery, technical workstreams, sponsor visibility, QA readiness, pilot validation logic, and transition toward commercial scale. The PM value is the control system that made complex delivery work visible, prioritized, and reviewable.

Recovery context

The work followed reliability and readiness gaps from a prior product stage. The delivery problem was to connect redesign and recovery work to structured QA readiness, pilot validation, sponsor-facing decisions, and scale-transition evidence.

Pavlo’s PM role

  • Coordinated delivery rhythm across technical contributors and workstreams.
  • Maintained visibility over risks, issues, dependencies, and readiness gaps.
  • Structured sponsor-facing status so delivery progress and blockers were easier to inspect.
  • Supported prioritization and change visibility, including MoSCoW-style prioritization where appropriate.
  • Helped connect recovery and redesign work to QA readiness, pilot validation, and scale-transition decisions.

Pilot readiness logic

Pilot logic was treated as validation under different operating conditions, not only as a launch event. The work connected field-readiness questions to QA validation and sponsor-facing readiness evidence.
