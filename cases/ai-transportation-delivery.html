Executive summary

I made technical delivery visible enough to govern.

From 2022 to 2025, I managed delivery visibility across a field detection system, a monitoring platform for deployed units, and a micromobility violation module. I converted technical uncertainty into schedules, readiness gates, RAID controls, sponsor-facing decisions, QA visibility, and pilot-to-scale discipline.

The system combined AI-enabled detection, field hardware, warning components, projected road-marking visibility, connectivity, and Smart City / ITS integration dependencies.

Program phases

The program moved through three delivery phases, each with its own team composition, technical risk profile, and readiness challenges. Across all three, the operating need was the same: a clear governance layer showing what was ready, blocked, decision-dependent, or exposed to field-condition risk.

Phase 1 · 2022Field detection systemAI-enabled pedestrian crossing detection: hardware selection, dataset collection, prototype build, city pilot, feedback collection, and transition to scaled deployment with a coordinated installation crew.
Phase 2 · 2023–2024Equipment-monitoring platformA remote-diagnostics platform for the deployed field units: concept design, frontend and backend build, equipment connection, and client-facing testing and refinement.
Phase 3 · 2024–2025Micromobility violation moduleAn e-scooter violation-detection module built on the field system: dataset collection, detection refinement, and validation of hard cases such as walked versus ridden scooters.
My PM operating system

Delivery controls that kept technical work governable.

I adjusted the delivery mechanism to the work: milestone planning for the hardware-heavy build, Jira/Kanban for iterative software work, and sponsor-facing controls across both.

PlanSchedule and delivery rhythm

Built the initial milestone baseline in Microsoft Project, then shifted to Jira-based iteration as delivery became software-heavy.

ControlRAID and dependency visibility

Separated execution work from blocked, decision-dependent, and field-risk-exposed work.

ValidateQA and field readiness

Connected defects, test status, installation constraints, and pilot evidence to readiness gates.

DecideSponsor and change governance

Prepared status, impact analysis, decision packages, and change-board inputs for scope, schedule, and budget decisions.

My role was to keep interfaces, readiness, risks, and ownership visible across the system—not to claim engineering authorship.
