Executive summary

I made technical delivery visible enough to govern.

The project sat inside a transportation-safety and smart-infrastructure implementation context where field reliability, stakeholder visibility, installation readiness, and measurable deployment outcomes mattered. My value was not engineering authorship; it was converting technical uncertainty into delivery controls: workstream readiness, issue and risk visibility, sponsor-facing status, pilot-readiness logic, and scale-transition discipline.

The implementation environment included AI-enabled pedestrian, vehicle, and micromobility detection; warning components; projected road-marking visibility; lighting and signage elements; V2I-readiness considerations; and integration logic for Smart City / ITS environments.

Recovery context

I worked through reliability and readiness gaps across several technical streams: detection behavior, field hardware, QA validation, integration dependencies, and sponsor-controlled decisions. The operating need was a clear governance layer showing what was ready, blocked, decision-dependent, or exposed to field-condition risk.

Delivery architecture / technical workstreams

I kept the delivery view organized around the technical streams that had to become ready together. This is the PM signal: I did not need to own each engineering decision to make the system governable.

01Detection logicI coordinated delivery visibility around pedestrian, vehicle, and micromobility detection behavior, movement-direction logic, and readiness risks around false positives / false negatives.
02Field hardware readinessI helped structure readiness visibility across projection, lighting, warning signs, T7 signal elements, camera placement, and real-road visibility conditions.
03Violation / event logicI supported implementation governance around driver non-yield scenarios, micromobility crossing behavior, incident capture, and event/archive requirements.
04ITS / Smart City integrationI helped keep integration dependencies visible for regional ITS, Safe City, Smart City environments, and V2I-readiness considerations.
05Pilot environment readinessI helped connect validation logic to high-risk deployment contexts such as unregulated crossings, school zones, limited-visibility areas, transit stops, and other field-risk locations.
06Sponsor governanceI maintained visibility over readiness, risks, dependencies, decisions, and validation status so sponsor-facing execution could move from recovery toward pilot and scale transition.
