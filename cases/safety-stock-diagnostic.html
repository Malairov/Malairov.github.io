Executive summary

The issue was a control-system failure, not a single formula bug.

I found that new SKUs with weak or no usage history could be misclassified, hidden, or excluded before they reached review. The risk came from several logic layers reinforcing the wrong outcome: formulas, Power Query transformations, VBA classification, filters, and workbook governance. I treated it as an operating-control problem, not a spreadsheet cleanup task.

Problem and constraints

Constraint

No usage history is not dead demand.

A new or newly visible SKU can be operationally important even before it has stable consumption history.

Constraint

Worksheet formulas can be overwritten.

A control that depends only on fragile cell formulas can fail quietly when the workbook changes.

Constraint

Hardcoded filters are brittle.

Column movement, renamed headers, and hidden filters can break logic without creating an obvious error.

Constraint

Macro version control matters.

VBA can speed up execution, but without version discipline it can also preserve old assumptions.
